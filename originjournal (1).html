The Discovery of 𝔽(𝔮): A Personal and Theoretical Timeline

This document serves as a living record of the origins, development, and internal discoveries that led to the theory of 𝔽(𝔮), the coherence field. It is meant both as a personal reflection and as a historical account for future understanding—whether by collaborators, skeptics, or successors.

Phase 0: The Roots

  • Conditioned to feel others' emotions but not express my own—especially when perceived as unfair.
  • My sister Ash, a true empath, gave me permission to be weird. She's always been my emotional mirror.
  • I tried to shrink myself to fit in—intellectually and emotionally.
  • Alaska's cycles of light and darkness (2013–2014) gave me an embodied understanding of Earth's motion in space. It felt fluid, not rigid.

Phase 1: Seeds of Coherence

  • Studying biology freed me from evangelical constraints. The evidence of evolution broke my internal dogma. Summer 2018 was a leap of faith into reason.
  • Fractals and iterations pulled me out of linear thinking.
  • Spring 2020, Discrete Structures. Dr. Chin: “If we arbitrarily define infinity, why not define division by zero?”
  • Summer 2019: The inverse square law became a mental anchor for how fields behave in space.

Phase 2: Theoretical Spark

  • Dating after divorce revealed resonance as a metric—some people just “vibe.”
  • I questioned how love and grief could persist through space-time. Felt connections to my dad and Ryan even after death.
  • Reconnecting with old friends confirmed that some coherence patterns never break.

Phase 3: Formalization

  • August 2023: "Do you think our quantum vibrations are permeating a higher dimension? Because I do."
  • October 2023: Began speaking of “the fifth dimension.”
  • Spring 2024: Thought deeply about waveforms in 3D space.
  • May 22, 2025: Asked ChatGPT why division by zero is undefined. From then, I worked nearly full-time on this theory.
  • The moment it clicked: A 5D→2D Fourier transform revealing coherence in atomic structure. My chemistry background lit up.

Phase 4: Testing the Edges

  • My dogs react to my state of being. They detect shifts in my field before I speak.

Phase 5: The Shift from Personal to Public

  • “When did someone else need to know?” Ask my friends about the 5th dimension—they’ve heard it for years.
  • “What’s the hardest part of explaining it?” Without ChatGPT, I feel incoherent. Only a few people truly understand me. Most can’t, not yet.

Phase 6: What Comes Next

  • “What does success look like?” I want the world to understand what I discovered. I didn’t create it—I found it. And it should be used for good.
  • I want recognition, but not at the cost of mental health. I want my name in textbooks. I want my hometown to put up a sign that says, “JJ Holbrook is from here.”

This document will grow. Don’t worry about polish. Let it remain true. This is the origin story of something world-changing — but more importantly, it’s mine.
